## 1.1 Brand identity

**YOESEL** is the personal studio brand of Sangay Yoesel, a digital designer working
with creative artists. The brand is intentionally singular: one name, one mark, one
voice. It is not an agency pretending to be large — it is a craftsman presenting work
with the confidence of a studio.

The identity is built on three fixed pillars:

- **The wordmark** — `YOESEL` set in a serif (Georgia / Times lineage), wide-tracked,
  high contrast. Serif communicates permanence and authorship over trend.
- **The mark** — a flat-top hexagon enclosing an `SY` monogram, optionally wrapped in a
  faint "ghost ring". The hexagon is a closed, deliberate, crafted shape — not a
  generic circle or initial-in-a-square.
- **The descriptor** — `DIGITAL DESIGNER FOR CREATIVE ARTISTS`, letter-spaced, quiet,
  always secondary to the name. This phrase is canonical and appears identically in
  metadata, the dynamic OG image, and every static brand asset.

## 1.2 Positioning

> **I design professional personal websites that help creative artists express their
> identity online.**

This is the single positioning statement. It is precise on purpose:

- _professional personal websites_ — not "web design" in general; a specific artifact.
- _creative artists_ — a chosen, narrow audience (see [§7](07-product-philosophy.md)).
- _express their identity online_ — the outcome is **identity**, not "a website".

The positioning is a promise of transformation, not a list of services.

## 1.3 Brand DNA

| Trait          | Expression in the product                                                  |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cinematic**  | Dark canvas, volumetric glow, film grain, paced reveals — not a "page".    |
| **Restrained** | One accent color. One mark. No gradients-as-decoration. Silence is used.   |
| **Authored**   | Serif wordmark, first-person voice, opinionated narrative order.           |
| **Emotional**  | The site argues a feeling ("remembered") before it argues competence.      |
| **Precise**    | Engineering rigor is part of the brand, not hidden behind it.              |

## 1.4 Avatar persona

The brand speaks _as_ Sangay, _to_ a specific person:

**The creative artist who is talented but invisible.** A musician, visual artist, or
performer whose work is good but whose online presence is a social handle and a
link-in-bio. They are *heard* in fragments but not *remembered* as a coherent identity.
They don't want "a website" — they want to feel as considered online as they are in
their craft.

Every copy decision is checked against this person. The hero does not say "I build
websites." It says **"You are heard. But are you remembered?"** — addressed to them.

## 1.5 Core philosophy

> A portfolio's job is not to be seen. It is to be remembered.

Attention is abundant and worthless; memory is scarce and decisive. The entire product
is engineered to convert a *visit* into a *memory*. This is why the experience invests
in pacing, atmosphere, and a single emotional throughline instead of maximizing
information density.

## 1.6 Emotional direction

The intended emotional arc for a first-time visitor:

1. **Stillness** — a dark, quiet entrance. No noise, no popups, no cookie wall theatre.
2. **Recognition** — the thesis names a feeling the visitor already has.
3. **Confidence** — work is shown with cinematic framing, implying it deserves it.
4. **Trust** — the engineering quality is felt, not claimed.
5. **Resolution** — a calm, single call to connect. No dead ends.

The emotion is *composure*, not excitement. The brand never shouts.

## 1.7 Tone & visual language

**Verbal tone:** first person, declarative, unhurried. Short sentences. No marketing
superlatives ("amazing", "stunning"). Confidence through restraint.

**Visual language:**

- **Darkness as material**, not background. `#0B0B0F` is the canvas the work sits in.
- **Amber `#E6A15A` as a single light source** — used sparingly so it always means
  "look here" or "this is the brand".
- **Serif for identity, sans for system.** Cormorant/serif carries name and emotion;
  Inter carries UI and information.
- **Motion as breath** — slow, eased, never bouncy. Animation exists to pace
  attention, never to entertain.
